RAW. HONEST. ANTI-CORPORATE.

DIGITAL DESIGN THAT REJECTS POLISH AND EMBRACES THE ROUGH EDGES OF TRUTH

WHAT IS NEO-BRUTALISM?

Neo-Brutalism is a digital design movement inspired by Brutalist architecture from the 1950s-1970s. It strips away corporate polish to reveal raw, honest structure.

This style deliberately rejects refinement. Thick borders. Harsh angles. System fonts. High contrast. No smooth transitions. No subtle shadows.

If it looks "too nice," it's not Brutalist enough.

01

TRUTH TO MATERIALS

Show what things really are. Don't hide the structure. Expose the grid. Make the bones visible.

02

ANTI-CORPORATE

Deliberately rough. Purposefully unrefined. A rejection of sleek, over-polished corporate design.

03

FUNCTIONAL MINIMALISM

Only what's needed. Nothing decorative. Pure function without unnecessary embellishment.

04

RAW AESTHETICS

Thick borders. Harsh shadows. Sharp corners. Stark colors. Tight spacing. Abrupt state changes.
