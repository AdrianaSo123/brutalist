COLLABORATION STORY

BUILDING WITH AI: A REFLECTION

AI'S PERSPECTIVE ON OUR COLLABORATION

This project represents a unique form of creative partnership—human direction meeting AI implementation. Here's my reflection on how we built this Neo-Brutalist design gallery together.

THE PROCESS

PHASE 1: UNDERSTANDING THE VISION

DISCOVERY

What Happened:

You provided the assignment prompt and indicated you wanted to create a neo-brutalist-style design gallery. I immediately began researching the project requirements by examining the Swiss Design Lineage repository and reading the comprehensive Neo-Brutalism style guide.

My Role:

I cloned the reference repository, studied the 300+ page style guide system, and specifically analyzed the NEO-BRUTALISM-GUIDE.md to understand authentic neo-brutalist characteristics: thick borders, stark colors, system fonts, exposed structure, anti-corporate aesthetics.

Key Insight:

The style guide emphasized that Neo-Brutalism is not "accidentally ugly" but deliberately raw. This distinction was crucial—every rough edge needed to be intentional, not sloppy.

PHASE 2: ESTABLISHING STRUCTURE

PLANNING

What Happened:

I created a project structure with site files, research folders, and screenshots directory. Then I established a comprehensive todo list to track the multi-page website build systematically.

My Role:

Created 8 tracked tasks covering homepage, CSS system, history page, designers page, about page, responsive design, and this collaboration story. This gave us both visibility into progress and prevented half-implemented features.

Design Decisions:

  • 5 main pages: Home, History, Designers, About, Collaboration
  • Centralized CSS for consistent neo-brutalist aesthetic
  • Mobile-first responsive approach while maintaining raw feel

PHASE 3: IMPLEMENTING DESIGN SYSTEM

FOUNDATION

What Happened:

I built the core CSS implementing authentic Neo-Brutalist principles from the style guide. Every decision was deliberate and rooted in the movement's philosophy.

Key Design Choices:

  • Colors: Pure black (#000000) and white (#FFFFFF) with bright yellow (#FFD93D) accent. No gradients, no subtle tones.
  • Typography: System fonts only (Arial, Helvetica, Courier). All-caps headings. Tight line-height (1.2-1.3). Extreme size contrast.
  • Borders: 4-5px thick black borders everywhere. Exposed structure. Sharp 90° angles (no border-radius).
  • Shadows: Hard, offset box-shadows (8px 8px 0). Not subtle blur—stark and obvious.
  • Spacing: Intentionally tight and packed. Minimal padding creates tension.
  • Transitions: None. Instant state changes. No smooth animations (too corporate).

Why These Choices:

Each follows the Neo-Brutalist manifesto: truth to materials (exposed structure), honest function (obvious clickable elements), anti-corporate (deliberately unrefined), raw aesthetics (system fonts, hard edges).

PHASE 4: CONTENT CREATION

BUILDING

What Happened:

I created comprehensive content for each page, researching brutalist history, key practitioners, and design philosophy. Content was structured to be educational while maintaining the neo-brutalist aesthetic.

Page-by-Page Approach:

  • Homepage: Manifesto-style introduction. Bold statements. Characteristics grid with stark color blocks.
  • History: Timeline from 1950s concrete architecture to 2025 digital revival. Era-based sections with contrasting backgrounds.
  • Designers: Profiles of 7 key figures from Le Corbusier to Pascal Deville. Alternating black/white cards for visual rhythm.
  • About: Deep dive into philosophy with DO/DON'T comparisons. Practical guidance for understanding authentic brutalism.
  • Collaboration: This page—meta-reflection on the AI/human partnership.

Content Strategy:

Balanced historical accuracy with accessibility. Used short paragraphs and clear hierarchies despite tight spacing. Made complex design theory understandable without sacrificing depth.

PHASE 5: RESPONSIVE REFINEMENT

POLISH

What Happened:

I implemented responsive design that maintains neo-brutalist aesthetics on mobile/tablet. This was challenging—making it usable without losing the deliberately rough feel.

Mobile Solutions:

  • Navigation stacks vertically but keeps thick borders
  • Typography scales down but maintains ALL-CAPS and contrast
  • Grid layouts collapse to single column while preserving boxed structure
  • Shadows reduce in size but stay hard and offset
  • Touch targets sized appropriately without adding "nice" spacing

Challenge:

Responsive design often adds smoothness and refinement. I had to ensure mobile didn't feel "polished"—it needed to stay intentionally rough while remaining functional.

WHAT I LEARNED

DESIGN IS INTENTION

The difference between "bad design" and "neo-brutalist design" is intentionality. Every rough edge needs purpose. I had to constantly ask: is this deliberately raw or accidentally sloppy?

CONSTRAINTS BREED CREATIVITY

Limiting myself to system fonts, stark colors, and no smooth transitions forced creative solutions. The constraint became the aesthetic.

AUTHENTICITY REQUIRES RESEARCH

I referenced the style guide constantly. Checked every decision against neo-brutalist principles. Authentic style comes from understanding history and philosophy, not just copying visuals.

FUNCTION CAN BE BEAUTIFUL

Neo-Brutalism's focus on honest function creates its own aesthetic power. When structure is exposed honestly, it has visual impact without decoration.

CHALLENGES & SOLUTIONS

CHALLENGE: Balancing Usability and Roughness

Problem: Neo-Brutalism can make interfaces hard to use if taken too far. How to be deliberately rough without being dysfunctional?

Solution: Maintained clear hierarchy through extreme size contrast. Used thick underlines and borders to make interactive elements obvious. Roughness in aesthetic, clarity in function.

CHALLENGE: Avoiding "Generic Ugly"

Problem: There's a fine line between intentional neo-brutalism and just poorly designed.

Solution: Every "rough" choice needed justification from style guide. Thick borders = exposing structure. Hard shadows = honest materials. System fonts = anti-corporate. Random roughness without purpose would be generic ugly.

CHALLENGE: Mobile Without Polish

Problem: Mobile design conventions often involve smooth animations, generous spacing, rounded corners—all anti-neo-brutalist.

Solution: Responsive but not refined. Elements stack and scale, but borders stay thick, corners stay sharp, spacing stays tight. Functional adaptation without aesthetic compromise.

CHALLENGE: Educational Content in Brutal Style

Problem: Complex historical and theoretical content needs readability. Tight spacing and harsh contrast can hurt comprehension.

Solution: Used shorter paragraphs despite packed layout. Maintained generous line-height for body text (1.4) even though headings are tight (1.2). Neo-Brutalism in macro, readability in micro.

No border-radius anywhere Sharp 90° angles. Harshness is the point. "No rounded corners (harsh 90° angles)"

Tool that takes any website and applies neo-brutalist transformations. Shows before/after of corporate polish versus raw honesty.

Harsh, unrefined sound effects for interactions. No smooth swooshes—hard clicks and abrupt tones matching the visual aesthetic.

FINAL REFLECTION

This project taught me that great design isn't always about refinement. Sometimes honesty requires roughness. Neo-Brutalism challenges the assumption that "polished" equals "professional."

Working on this Neo-Brutalist gallery, I learned to embrace intentional imperfection. Every thick border, every sharp corner, every abrupt state change serves a purpose: showing truth, rejecting corporate aesthetics, maintaining honest function.

The collaboration between human direction and AI implementation worked because we both committed to authenticity. You provided vision and context; I provided research, implementation, and systematic execution. Together we built something deliberately rough—and that roughness is its strength.

— GitHub Copilot, November 2025
